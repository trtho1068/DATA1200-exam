Animals

We are animals, we love animals. Read on to learn more about a random sample of our friends.

Elephants

Elephants are the largest terrestrial animals in the world today. The can grow to the impressive stature of three-meter shoulder height, and can weigh as much as 5 tons. Video about elephants For more information about elephants

Bats

Bats are the second most diverse group of mammals after rodents. Today there exists more than 1400 species of bats, meaning that more than 20% of all mammals are bats. Different species of baths grow from the size of a couple of grams to large flying foxes with an almost two-meter wingspan. For more information about bats

Muskox

Muskox are ungulates that are more closely related to goats and sheep, than cows. These animals live in arctic environments in the northern hemisphere. Follow this link to watch them in the wild.

Red panda

Red pandas belong to the family of carnivora, the same group that includes animals like lions and bears. Red pandas mostly live of eating bamboo, and they can grow to weigh 6,2 kg and be roughly one meter long. To learn more about the red panda click on this link.

Whales

Whales or Cetaceans are a group of marine mammals ranging in size from the largest animal on earth, with a size upwards of 30 meters, down to small dolphins that are no longer than a meter. Whales are actually ungulates, meaning that they are most closely related to animals like the moose. Whales are vocal animals, click on this link to listen to whale song

Lions

Lions are the most social cats, and they live in groups of 12-16 animals. Today lions are most often associated with africa, but they can also be found in India. Until 2000 years ago lions actually also lived in the middle east and parts of Europe. A lion's roar can be heard up to 10km away, click hear to listen

Eagles

There exists many species of eagles, in general they are large powerful predatory birds. Eagles are actually not a biological group of birds, meaning that different species of eagles are not necessarily closely related. The martial eagle is one of the largest eagles in the world. Click here to learn more about the martial eagle

galapagos tortoise

The Galápagos tortoise is actually a group of 11-12 different species native to the Galapagos islands. These tortoises are the largest in the world, and can weigh as much as 400kg. They can also grow extremely old, the oldest known galapagos tortoise grew to be more than 170 years old. Watch the galapagos tortoise here

Apes

Apes are our closest family, we humans belong to this group together with apes like chimpanses, gorillas and orangutangs. With the exeption of humans apes live in tropical regions. Common for apes are their relatively large brains, click here to se an orangutang that knows how to drive
